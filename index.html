Why Indoor Air?

Humans spend most of their time indoors.

Humans spend about 90% of their time indoors, where concentrations of some pollutants are 2 to 5 times higher than in typical outdoor concentrations.

Many buildings have bad indoor air quality.

Many buildings, both new and old, struggle with indoor air quality, as old buildings tend to have outdated ventilation systems and toxins trapped in the home, while newer buildings have a tendency to lack sufficient mechanical ventilation that keeps airborne pollutants circulating in the air in exchange for energy efficient buildings.

We breathe a lot of air, and there is a lot in that air.

The average person breathes 16,000 quarts of air everyday, and this air has ~70,000 visible and invisible particles floating in it. Also, nowadays the amount of serious airborne diseases (such as COVID-19) increases, so proper indoor air filtration is vital to human health.
